CLEAR

The NASA Space Weather Center of Excellence CLEAR aims to build a comprehensive prediction framework for solar energetic particles (SEPs), addressing one of the most critical challenges in space weather forecasting.

Visit us on the NASA space weather program website at: https://science.nasa.gov/clear/

Vision

The CLEAR Center will deliver robust, quantifiable nowcasts and forecasts of space radiation levels up to 24 hours in advance. It will provide:

  • Probabilistic forecasts of solar eruptive events before they occur.
  • Nowcasts and forecasts of SEP event properties, including onset, intensity, spectra, and timing.
  • All-clear forecasts predicting periods with SEP intensities below critical thresholds.

Motivation

SEPs pose severe risks to astronauts, aviation, and satellites. For example, >100 MeV protons increase astronaut exposure inside spacecraft, while >500 MeV protons can penetrate Earth’s atmosphere and endanger aviation. With upcoming Artemis lunar missions and future deep-space exploration, timely and accurate SEP forecasts are essential to protect human and technological assets.

Objectives

  • Develop, test, and validate a modular “plug-and-play” SEP forecast framework that integrates observational data, physics-based models, empirical models, and machine learning.
  • Build a benchmark SEP dataset with standard evaluation metrics for validation and community use.
  • Deliver prediction capabilities that advance the state of the art in all five SEP forecasting gap areas (pre-eruption probabilities, onset/peak timing, intensity profiles, spectra, and all-clear periods).

Integrated Multi-Model Framework

CLEAR will deliver the modular, plug-and-play SEP forecast system that unites:

  • Physics-based models (e.g., AWSoM-R for solar wind, EEG for CME initiation, M-FLAMPA/PARISAN for particle acceleration and transport).
  • Machine learning approaches trained on decades of SEP, CME, and flare data to extend forecasts into the pre-eruption phase.
  • Empirical models (e.g., SEPSTER, RELeASE) calibrated and enhanced with real-time solar data.

This integrated framework will provide forecasts with quantifiable uncertainties and up to 24-hour lead time, far exceeding current capabilities.

Transformative Impact on Space Weather Forecasting

CLEAR directly advances all five SEP forecast areas identified in NASA’s Space Weather Science and Measurement Gap Analysis:

  • Pre-eruption “all-clear” predictions.
  • Onset time forecasts.
  • Peak intensity estimates.
  • Event-integrated intensity profiles.
  • Probabilistic post-event forecasts.

Impact of CLEAR on Space Exploration

  • Supports NASA’s Artemis lunar missions and future crewed deep-space travel.
  • Enhances operational readiness for aviation and satellite operators by reducing risk from radiation hazards.
  • Provides reliable all-clear forecasts, critical for planning astronaut EVAs and launch windows.

Relevance and Impacts

CLEAR will foster a multi-/inter-/trans-disciplinary environment, supporting students, postdocs, and early-career scientists. Outreach will include workshops, seminars, and public communication.
